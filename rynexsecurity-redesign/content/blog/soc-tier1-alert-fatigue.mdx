Most breaches that make headlines weren't invisible. Somewhere in a SIEM, an alert fired. The
problem is rarely a missing detection — it's a Tier-1 analyst buried under thousands of
low-fidelity alerts, unable to tell the one that matters from the background noise.

## Why alert volume alone is a vanity metric

A SOC that proudly reports "50,000 events monitored this month" is telling you nothing about
outcomes. The number that matters is mean time to detect a genuine incident, and how much of an
analyst's day is spent triaging noise versus investigating real signal.

## What actually reduces fatigue

1. **Correlation over volume** — rules that combine weak signals (unusual login time + new
   device + impossible travel) into one high-confidence alert, instead of three separate tickets.
2. **Tuning as a continuous process**, not a one-time setup step after the SIEM goes live.
3. **Clear escalation playbooks** so Tier-1 analysts know exactly when to hand off, instead of
   sitting on an ambiguous alert until it ages out.

A SOC's value isn't how much it watches. It's how fast it turns a genuine signal into a
contained incident — and how little of an analyst's attention gets wasted getting there.
